Part VI: The Human & Societal Dimension

The Future of Work & Education

AI is rapidly reshaping the labor market, demanding new skills at an unprecedented pace and widening the wage gap, while educational institutions struggle to keep up.

  • 66% faster core skill change for jobs highly exposed to AI.
  • 56% wage premium for AI-skilled peers, more than double from last year (25%).
  • 92% of undergrads now use generative AI for their studies.

Governance, Risk, and Trust

Regulatory bodies are accelerating efforts to govern AI, while public sentiment remains divided and practical workplace risks become primary concerns.

  • East-West Divide: 83% of Chinese view AI positively vs. 39% in the U.S.
  • Top Employee Concern: Cybersecurity risks (51%), followed by hallucinations (50%).
  • 9x increase in global legislative mentions of AI since 2016.
