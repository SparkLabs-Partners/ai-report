A Detailed Analysis of H1 2025

Chapter 14: The Future of Work & Education

AI is acting as a powerful catalyst for change in the labor market, initiating a "Great Skills Reshuffle." The demand for AI-related skills is skyrocketing, creating a significant wage premium. Meanwhile, educational institutions are struggling to adapt their policies and training to this new reality.

The Great Skills Reshuffle

66% Faster

Rate of change for core skills in jobs highly exposed to AI, more than double the rate from one year prior.

56% Wage Premium

Commanded by workers with specialized AI skills, up from 25% last year.

Education's Uneven Response

While students are rapidly adopting AI tools for their studies (92% of undergraduates), formal institutional support lags significantly behind.

  • Over 50% of K-12 teachers report their schools have no formal policy on AI use.
  • 56% of K-12 teachers have received no formal training on using AI tools.

Chapter 15: Governance, Risk, and Trust

As AI becomes more integrated into society, governance and risk management are moving to the forefront. Regulatory activity is accelerating globally, while public opinion shows a stark East-West divide. In the workplace, abstract fears are giving way to practical concerns about data security and accuracy.

The Global Regulatory Acceleration

  • Legislative mentions of AI increased ninefold since 2016 across 75 countries.
  • US federal agencies introduced 59 new AI regulations in 2024, more than double the previous year.
  • 24 US states have passed regulations targeting deepfakes in elections.

Public Sentiment & Workplace Risks

While large majorities in Eastern nations like China (83%) view AI as beneficial, skepticism persists in Western nations like the U.S. (39%). Employee concerns are focused on practical risks.

  • 51%: Cybersecurity risks
  • 50%: Factual inaccuracies
  • 43%: Data privacy threats
