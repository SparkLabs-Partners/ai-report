Chapter 1: The Venture Capital Barbell: A Market of Extremes

The H1 2025 venture landscape is defined by a stark contrast: a boom in mega-deals and seed-stage funding, which squeezes traditional mid-stage venture investments. AI-focused startups captured an impressive $165 billion (53%) of the total $310 billion in global VC funding.

The Squeezed Middle: Venture-Scale Contraction

While top-line funding numbers are high, the number of individual deals has fallen to a five-year low of just 2,101 in Q1 2025, a marked decrease from 2,516 in Q1 2024 and a steep drop from 3,022 in Q1 2022.

The Other End of the Barbell: The Seed-Stage

The share of new VC fund launches dedicated to AI is projected to reach 24.5% in 2025, a massive increase from just 5.4% in 2022. Y Combinator's Winter 2025 batch saw 82% of its startups being AI-related.

Chapter 2: Corporate & Private Equity Strategy

Corporations and PE firms have become major forces in the AI landscape, doubling down on investments and acquisitions to integrate AI capabilities. Corporate-backed startup funding more than doubled to over $129 billion in H1 2025.

  • CVCs & direct corporate investments account for 47% of all deal value.
  • 64% of leaders plan to use M&A to acquire AI talent.
  • 95% of PE firms plan to multiply their AI investments.

Chapter 3: Market Projections and Economic Impact

The economic footprint of AI is set to expand dramatically. Projections show a path to a multi-trillion dollar market, with generative AI alone expected to grow at a staggering 44.2% CAGR to reach over $1 trillion by 2034.

Macroeconomic Impact
